// 01_THE_COMPLIANCE_GAP

Why Wearables Fail the Vulnerable.

60% Rejection Rate

Patients with dementia often remove wearables due to sensory irritation or confusion. A device on the bedside table cannot detect a fall in the bathroom.

Alarm Fatigue

Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors generate massive false positives. Caregivers eventually stop reacting to the noise of "dumb" sensors.

The Dignity Dilemma

Families are forced to choose between safety (invasive cameras) and privacy. This leaves high-risk areas like bathrooms unmonitored.

// 02_THE_SCIENCE

Beyond Deep Learning.

Standard AI relies on massive cloud datasets. Hakilix uses Neuromorphic Computing—mimicking the biological structure of the human brain to process data at the edge with extreme efficiency.

Spiking Neural Networks

We use Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) models. Information is encoded in the timing of spikes, not the magnitude, reducing power consumption by 100x compared to CNNs.

Causal Discovery

Structural Causal Models (SCM) allow the system to reason counterfactually: "Did the subject fall, or did they lie down intentionally?" This eliminates context-blind errors.

Homomorphic Encryption

We utilize Federated Learning on encrypted gradients. The global model learns from local events without ever decrypting or exposing raw patient data.

System FAQ

Does Hakilix use cameras?

No. We use mmWave radar and low-res thermal sensors. No optical images are ever captured.

Does it work in the dark?

Yes. Both radar and thermal sensors operate independently of light conditions, ensuring 24/7 protection.

What happens during a power cut?

The Hakilix Node includes a backup battery and cellular (LTE-M) failover for critical alerts.
